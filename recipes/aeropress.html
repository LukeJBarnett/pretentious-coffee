What is a Aeropress

Back in 2005 the president of Aerobie, Mr Alan Adler invented the Aerobie Aeropress (who is apparently also a fan of alliteration). The Aeropress is a filter styled coffee maker which produces a clean and bright flavour much like a pour over. It is made up of four parts. ish.

  • A couple of nesting cylinders that you push to apply pressure for extraction.
  • A cap made from a thick plastic (easy to lose)
  • A filter which will be either paper or metal. The plastic itself is BPA free but what isn’t these days?

The Aeropress generally produces stronger as the coffee itself ‘steeps’ for a longer period before extraction. Similar to a plunger/french press. When extracting it requires a fair bit of elbow grease depending on your dosage so I wouldn’t recommend buying one of these for your old Gran.

How To Actually Make an Aeropress

  1. Disregard all factory instructions. They are BAD.
  2. Put plunger in bottom of cylinder (hehe) and stand up on the plunger end.
  3. Fill with 15g of coffee which is about a heaped scoop. Then put "some" water in, enough to be able to stir
  4. Agitate (the fancy word for stiring) for about 10 seconds.
  5. Fill the rest of the chamber and screw lid on.
  6. Pull chamber down far enough that coffee starts to push through the cap at the top.
  7. Leave to steep for 2 mins
  8. pop onto a cup, cap side down and then lean hard on it to push the steeped coffee through the filter and the cap.
  9. Enjoy! (dont add milk you animal)
